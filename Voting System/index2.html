About

Voting is a method for a group, such as a meeting or an electorate, to make a collective decision or express an opinion, usually following discussions, debates, or election campaigns.

Democracies elect holders of high office by voting. Residents of a place represented by an elected official are called constituents, and those constituents who cast a ballot for their chosen candidate are called voters. While many systems are used for decision-making, only those that support proportional representation can be used in elections.
